Adopting a Dog

1. Take your time before taking a decision

Sometimes adopting an adorable puppy can be a quick decision, but you should avoid that. Understand that this is a big commitment and should not be done withouth taking many things in consideration



2. Having a dog is a costly decision, make sure your finances are ready for that.

The first mandatory shots at the veterinary, buying a cute doggy bed and first food is just the beggining of the exepenses. The dog will require constant care, including buying new dog toys, beds, veterinary visits, continuos supply of food and more. Also, the leash, dog clothing(if needed), collar and other gadgets might cost more than expected.



3. Consider if you are willing to have your dog spayed or neutered.

There are so many unwanted dogs in the shelters, that their excessive breeding has to be contained. Think of decisions to make sure your dog does not get pregnant or inpregnate other dog.



4. Make sure your dog has an identification tag or a chip

If in an unfortunate even your dog gets lost, an identification tag or a micro chip can be an amazing tool to make sure your dog is found. If your dog gets lost and does not have any of those, it will be way harder to find the dog.



5. Humans brush teeth, why shouldn't the dogs also?

Dogs dental health is very important. Make sure you brush your dog's teeth atleast once a week. Make sure it is a pleasurable activity for the dog by introducing it slowly and with a lot of treats. Another way to achieve this is to provide the dog with special teeth brushing dog bones.



6. Start your dog training as soon as possible

Dog are extremely sucebtable to training in their early ears. Make sure the training starts at the young age. Consider taking dog training classes for better results. The training should not be only tricks, it should include basic complience and socialization.

Adopting a Cat

1. Cats are very teritorial animals, make sure they have their own territory

In the first days or weeks of your new cat arrival, make sure the cat has some place to call her own. This all new space might be confusing for your cat, so make sure she has her own hideout or a place where she will not be disturbed too much. A small room would be a perfect place.



2. Make sure your cat has a litter box

Before bringing the cat home buy a litter box and place it in some private place where the cat can do her business withouth disturbance Do some research on cat litter to make sure you buy the one that suits your needs.



3. Set up a place for cat food and drink

Make sure the place to eat is further away from the litter box. Once you choose a place, try to make it constant, so the cat would learn of the place where she can always get some food and drink. Choose the size of the bowls accordingly to your cats size.



4. Cat claws require them to be used, make sure it is not furniture

Cats scratch their claws on random things, this is in their nature. Buy some special cat scratchers to make sure cat is not sharpening her claws on your furniture. There is a wide variety of scratchers of different materials and sizes. Play around with these models to make sure the cat finds what it likes the most.

5. Imagine yourself being a cat

Look around your home in the cat's eyes. There might be a lot of places the cat can get to, where you would not image. Make sure there are no hazardous items on top of the objects, as cupboards, shelves or wardrobes. Also, make sure there are no air wents or other dangerous holes in the house that the cat could enter.



6. Make some ground rules with other family members

If there are other people in your home, make sure everybody is aware of some ground rules how to treat the cat. Animals love consistency, so make sure everybody is aware of the private space the cat needs, the way it should be treated and other things. Once everybody is aware of this, the cat should interact with everybody in the house accordingly and not choose it's mortal enemy.
